## Manager Role Overview

### Your Responsibilities

As a Manager, you:
- **Oversee Assigned Creators**: Monitor their onboarding and progress
- **Conduct Meetings**: Schedule and complete introductory meetings
- **Review Content**: Approve or reject creator uploads
- **Manage Availability**: Set your meeting availability schedule
- **Coordinate Shoots**: Manage studio shoot assignments
- **Handle Commitments**: Assign and track weekly content tasks

### What You CAN Do

✅ View all assigned creators  
✅ Review and manage creator applications  
✅ Schedule and complete meetings  
✅ Review and approve content uploads  
✅ Assign weekly commitments  
✅ Manage studio shoots  
✅ View creator contracts  
✅ Access email logs for your actions  

### What You CANNOT Do

❌ Create other manager/admin accounts  
❌ Modify user roles  
❌ Access creators not assigned to you  
❌ Generate contracts  
❌ Manage system-wide settings  
❌ Delete user accounts

## Managing Assigned Creators

### Viewing Your Creators

1. Navigate to **Creators** tab in manager dashboard
2. See list of creators assigned to you
3. View their:
   - Name and contact info
   - Onboarding status (X/10 steps completed)
   - Access level (no_access, meeting_only, full_access)
   - Meeting status
   - Contract status

### Creator Lifecycle

**Application → Meeting → Onboarding → Contract → Active Creator**

1. **Application Received**
   - Admin reviews and approves
   - Creator gets `meeting_only` access
   - Automatically assigned to you

2. **Meeting Booking**
   - Creator books meeting from available slots
   - You receive email notification
   - Meeting shows in your calendar

3. **Meeting Completion**
   - You mark meeting as complete
   - Creator automatically gets `full_access`
   - Can now complete all 10 onboarding steps

4. **Onboarding Progress**
   - Monitor creator's step completion
   - Answer questions during process
   - Ensure profile accuracy

5. **Contract Signing**
   - Admin generates contract
   - Creator signs digitally
   - Becomes active creator

6. **Ongoing Management**
   - Review weekly commitments
   - Approve content uploads
   - Coordinate studio shoots

## Availability Management

### Setting Your Schedule

1. Go to **Availability** section
2. Click **Manage Availability**
3. Set weekly schedule:
   - Select days of week
   - Set start/end times for each day
   - Define meeting duration (default: 60 min)

### Availability Presets

Create quick templates:
- **Weekday Mornings**: Mon-Fri, 9:00-12:00
- **Afternoon Slots**: Mon-Fri, 14:00-17:00
- **Weekend Availability**: Sat-Sun, 10:00-15:00

### Blocking Specific Dates

For vacation or busy days:
1. Go to **Block Dates** tab
2. Select date range
3. Add optional reason
4. Click **Block**
5. Creators won't see these dates as available

### Meeting Duration

- Default: 60 minutes
- Adjust per day of week if needed
- Buffer time automatically added between meetings

## Meeting Coordination

### When Creator Books Meeting

You receive:
- Email notification with details
- Dashboard notification badge
- Calendar entry (if integrated)

### Before the Meeting

**Prepare:**
- Review creator's application
- Check their onboarding progress (Steps 1-2 completed)
- Have meeting link ready (if online)
- Confirm location details (if in-person)

### Meeting Types

**Online Meetings:**
- Conducted via video call
- Meeting link provided in invitation
- Test tech 5 minutes before

**In-Person Meetings:**
- Held at specified location
- Address in meeting details
- Plan for 60-90 minutes

### During the Meeting

**Discussion Topics:**
- Creator's goals and expectations
- Bureau Boudoir process overview
- Content creation guidelines
- Answer questions
- Discuss boundaries and comfort levels
- Explain remaining 8 onboarding steps

### Completing the Meeting

1. Navigate to **Meetings** tab
2. Find the meeting
3. Click **Mark as Complete**
4. Add optional meeting notes
5. System automatically:
   - Grants creator `full_access`
   - Unlocks steps 3-10
   - Sends confirmation email to creator
   - Updates timeline

### Rescheduling Meetings

If creator requests reschedule:
1. Review reschedule request in dashboard
2. Select new date/time
3. Add reason for record
4. System sends updated invitation

## Content Review

### Reviewing Uploads

1. Go to **Content Review** tab
2. View pending content:
   - Photos
   - Videos
   - Linked to commitments or shoots
3. Preview files
4. Check descriptions and metadata

### Approval Criteria

✅ **Approve if:**
- Content quality meets standards
- Appropriate for platform
- Matches commitment description
- No copyright/legal issues
- Creator appears comfortable

❌ **Reject if:**
- Poor quality (lighting, focus, etc.)
- Inappropriate content
- Violates platform guidelines
- Copyright concerns
- Creator appears uncomfortable

### Approval Process

1. Click on content item
2. Review file and details
3. Click **Approve** or **Reject**
4. If rejecting, provide clear feedback
5. Optional: Add marketing notes for approved content

### Content Types

- **Photo Sets**: Usually 20-50 images per set
- **Videos**: Various lengths (Solo, Duo, etc.)
- **Custom Content**: Creator-specific requests
- **PPV Content**: Premium pay-per-view items

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Creator Missed Meeting

**Steps:**
1. Check if creator contacted you
2. Send follow-up email
3. Offer to reschedule
4. Note in meeting record
5. If pattern develops, escalate to admin

### Scenario 2: Content Doesn't Meet Standards

**Steps:**
1. Reject content with specific feedback
2. Explain what needs improvement
3. Offer guidance or examples
4. Allow resubmission
5. Encourage communication if unclear

### Scenario 3: Creator Not Progressing

**Steps:**
1. Check their last activity
2. Send check-in message
3. Offer assistance with blockers
4. Review onboarding steps together
5. Escalate if no response after 1 week

### Scenario 4: Technical Issues

**Steps:**
1. Document the issue
2. Try basic troubleshooting
3. Check if widespread or isolated
4. Report to development team if needed
5. Keep creator informed of progress

### Scenario 5: Creator Has Concerns

**Steps:**
1. Listen attentively
2. Document concerns
3. Address what you can
4. Escalate to admin if needed
5. Follow up to ensure resolution

## FAQ

**Q: How many creators will I manage?**  
A: Typically 5-15 active creators, depending on workload and availability.

**Q: Can I decline a meeting request?**  
A: Yes, if it's outside your availability. Contact admin to reassign if needed.

**Q: What if a creator isn't a good fit?**  
A: Document concerns and discuss with admin. They can reassign or handle situation.

**Q: How long should content review take?**  
A: Average 2-3 minutes per item. Quality over speed.

**Q: Can I communicate with creators outside the platform?**  
A: Use platform messaging primarily. External communication for urgent matters only.

**Q: What if I need time off?**  
A: Block dates in your availability calendar at least 2 weeks in advance.
